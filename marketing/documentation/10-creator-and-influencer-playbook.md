# 10 — Creator & Influencer Playbook

ETHOS's growth strategy is built on **authentic creator partnerships**, not paid ads. The target audience — devout Christians aged 18-30 — trusts people over brands. They follow faith creators who live what they teach. This playbook defines who those creators are, how to engage them, and how to scale the creator ecosystem without becoming dependent on any single personality.

### Notable Quote

This reflects Imman's brand positioning: substance over clout. He actively pushes back against the "grind culture" creator economy and frames content creation as a spiritual discipline. This aligns with ETHOS's "For the person you're becoming" ethos — growth is internal, not performative.

### His Role in ETHOS

| Function | What He Does |
|----------|--------------|
| **Distribution** | Promotes ETHOS to his existing 150K+ audience through organic content integration. His audience is the initial funnel for early adopters. |
| **Content** | Creates a build-in-public series documenting ETHOS development. Day-in-the-life content showing how he personally uses the app (Daily Anchor in his morning routine, Chat Companion for real decisions, Radar Chart for weekly reflection). |
| **Face** | Embodies the target user persona. His framing: *"I'm not yet the person I want to be, but I'm far from who I once was."* This vulnerability is the emotional hook — he's not a finished product preaching from a pedestal; he's on the journey alongside the audience. |
| **Feedback loop** | Funnels audience input (DMs, comments, polls) directly to the product team. Serves as a real-time signal for what resonates and what doesn't. |

### Risks

| Risk | Severity | Detail |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Audience mismatch** | High | Imman's audience may skew toward self-improvement and entrepreneurship, not devout practicing Christians. The overlap exists but is unvalidated. A user who follows Imman for "how to build a business" may not be the same user who wants a daily Scripture companion. **This needs validation before assuming his audience = ETHOS ICP.** |
| **Single point of failure** | High | If Imman becomes THE product — if users associate ETHOS exclusively with him — the platform becomes a personal brand business, not a scalable product. Personal brand businesses have a ceiling: they can't grow beyond the founder's reach, and they collapse if the founder leaves. |
| **Content depth** | Medium | Imman's content has been described as "recycling faith-business platitudes." For the devout Christian ICP, surface-level inspiration is insufficient. The audience wants theological depth, scriptural rigor, and genuine vulnerability — not motivational posters with Bible verses. Imman's content needs to evolve toward resonance with the devout ICP specifically. |

### Strategic Boundary

**Imman should be positioned as "one of many leaders", not the centerpiece.**

Concrete guardrails:

- **Launch with 10-15 leaders** so that no single person dominates the platform's identity.
- **No leader should have >20% of total engagement** across the platform. If Imman's content accounts for more than 20% of interactions, the team must actively promote other leaders to rebalance.
- **The ETHOS brand must stand without him.** Test this by asking: "If Imman left tomorrow, would users still open the app?" If the answer is no, the brand is too dependent.
- **Marketing materials should lead with the product, not the person.** Imman can appear in content, but the hero is always the app experience — the Daily Anchor, the Chat Companion, the growth journey.

## 2. Creator Roster (Current)

| Creator | Platform | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|

| **@ekevvscrusade** | Instagram | Faith content |

### Status: Unvetted

These creators are noted as potential partners but have **NOT been vetted**. Before any outreach or engagement, the following checklist must be completed for each creator:

- [ ] **Verify audience demographics align with devout Christian ICP.** Use Instagram Insights (if they share) or third-party tools (HypeAuditor, Modash) to confirm their followers are 18-30, faith-oriented, and geographically relevant. An entrepreneurship creator with 90% male followers aged 25-44 is not the same as a faith creator with a balanced 18-30 audience.

- [ ] **Check content quality and theological alignment.** Review their last 30 posts and 10 stories. Are they scripturally accurate? Do they reflect the theological diversity ETHOS supports (non-denominational, broadly evangelical)? Are there any posts that would create brand risk (prosperity gospel, political extremism, theological positions that alienate segments of the ICP)?

- [ ] **Confirm Christ-imparted righteousness alignment, not earned-righteousness drift.** This is load-bearing for ETHOS positioning (see [`../guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES`](../guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES) — *The Foundation*). Does the creator preach that righteousness comes through Christ — or through grinding, "becoming more masculine," fitness-as-worship, or any other earned frame? Grindset Christianity is the most subtle and most dangerous misalignment in this niche; it sounds adjacent but works against the brand. Reject creators whose content frames manhood as the *price* of God's favor rather than the *fruit* of Christ's gift.

- [ ] **Confirm they don't slide into the feral or domesticated camps.** ETHOS speaks to the governed man — neither Tate-coded ("alpha," dominance, "high-value man") nor performatively soft ("you are enough," candle-lit devotional aesthetic). A creator who codes either way will pull the audience in the wrong direction even when their individual posts seem fine.

- [ ] **Assess engagement rate (not just follower count).** A creator with 50K followers and 5% engagement (2,500 interactions per post) is more valuable than one with 500K followers and 0.3% engagement (1,500 interactions per post). Target engagement rates above 3% for Instagram, above 5% for TikTok.

- [ ] **Confirm they would authentically use and promote ETHOS.** The worst outcome is a creator who posts one sponsored story and never mentions the app again. The best outcome is a creator who genuinely integrates ETHOS into their daily life and shares that organically. Before formalizing any partnership, give them the app for 2 weeks and see if they naturally engage with it.

### Vetting Process

1. **Initial research** (1-2 hours per creator): Review content, check audience demographics, note any red flags.
2. **Soft outreach** (Template A — see Section 5): Low-commitment intro. Gauge interest and authenticity.
3. **Product trial** (2 weeks): Give them full Pro access. Do not ask them to post anything. Just observe whether they use it.
4. **Debrief call** (30 minutes): What did they like? What didn't work? Would they recommend it to their audience without being asked?
5. **Partnership decision**: Only proceed if they passed all four checkpoints above AND demonstrated genuine engagement during the trial.

## 3. Founding Leader Recruitment (Phase 2)

### Target Profile

The ideal Founding Leader is not a "Christian influencer." They are a **proven professional who happens to be a devout Christian** and has built something worth learning from.

| Criteria | Minimum Bar | Why It Matters |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Business success** | $1M+ revenue business | Proves they have real expertise to share, not just opinions. Users are paying for mentorship from someone who has achieved what they aspire to. |
| **Social media presence** | 10K+ engaged followers | Ensures they can drive initial awareness and bring their own audience to the platform. |
| **Reputation** | Known for generosity and mentorship | The brand association must be aspirational in the right way — "this person helped me grow" not "this person showed me their Lamborghini." |
| **Diversity** | Across age, industry, denomination, gender | ETHOS serves a broad Christian audience. The leader roster must reflect that breadth. A roster of 15 white male tech founders in their 30s alienates the majority of the ICP. |

### The Pitch

> "We're building the premier platform for Christian entrepreneurs to receive mentorship from proven leaders like you. You'll have a direct channel to mentor the next generation, monetize your expertise, and build your brand — without the overhead of running your own platform. We handle tech, payments, community moderation. You focus on impact."

This pitch works because it addresses the three things leaders care about:

1. **Impact** — "mentor the next generation" (mission-aligned motivation)
2. **Revenue** — "monetize your expertise" (practical motivation)
3. **Simplicity** — "we handle tech, payments, community moderation" (removes the barrier that stops most leaders from building their own platform)

### The Offer

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Revenue split** | 70/30 favoring the leader. For every $29/mo subscription attributed to a leader, the leader receives $20.30 and ETHOS retains $8.70. |
| **Upfront cost** | Zero. No fees, no setup costs, no minimum commitments. |
| **Content ownership** | Leaders keep 100% ownership of their content and intellectual property. If they leave ETHOS, they take everything they created with them. |
| **Equity** | Founding Leaders receive equity options — small but meaningful. This aligns long-term incentives and makes them stakeholders, not just contractors. Exact terms to be defined by legal counsel based on number of founding leaders and company valuation. |

### Rollout Timeline

| Phase | Timeframe | Activity | Scale |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Private Beta** | Month 1-2 | Recruit 10-15 founding leaders using Imman's network and direct outreach. Invite 100 users (hand-selected, high-engagement free users). Test interaction formats, pricing, and leader tools. | 10-15 leaders, 100 users |
| **Public Launch** | Month 3-4 | Press push with the narrative: "Get mentored by the leaders you follow." Open Leaders tier to all Pro users. Feature founding leaders prominently in marketing. | 10-15 leaders, 500-1,000 users |
| **Scale** | Month 5-6 | Open leader applications with a vetting process. Target 50 leaders across diverse industries, denominations, and demographics. Introduce leader discovery and matching features. | 50 leaders, 2,000-5,000 users |

### Risks to Manage

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| **Cold start problem** | Leaders won't join without users; users won't pay without leaders. Classic two-sided marketplace chicken-and-egg. | Solve by recruiting 10-15 founding leaders **before launch** using Imman's personal network. These leaders commit based on mission alignment and equity upside, not current user numbers. Simultaneously, hand-select 100 engaged free users for the beta — people who have already demonstrated high engagement (daily Anchor opens, multiple chats, saved verses). |
| **Quality control** | Bad advice from a leader could lead to reputation damage, user harm, or legal liability. A leader giving unqualified financial advice or harmful theological guidance creates existential risk. | Solve with **strict vetting** (see Target Profile criteria), **invite-only access** (no open applications in Phase 1), and clear **Terms of Service** stating that leaders provide "mentorship, not professional or financial advice." Establish a reporting mechanism for users and a rapid response process for removing problematic leaders. |
| **Leader poaching** | Leaders extract their ETHOS audience to their own platforms (Patreon, Skool, email list). The platform becomes a lead-gen tool rather than a destination. | Solve by making the **on-platform economics more attractive** than off-platform alternatives. The 70/30 revenue share, zero operational overhead, and built-in audience make ETHOS the path of least resistance for monetizing mentorship. Additionally, the platform's tools (async Q&A, group calls, content library) should be best-in-class — leaders stay because the experience is better, not because they're contractually locked in. |
| **Imman dependency** | Imman becomes the #1 leader by a wide margin. If he leaves, the Leaders feature collapses. Users joined "for Imman," not "for ETHOS." | Solve by enforcing the **20% engagement cap** from day one. Actively promote other leaders in discovery, marketing, and app placement. If Imman consistently exceeds 20% of engagement, reduce his visibility in algorithmic recommendations and increase promotion of other leaders. The goal is a diversified ecosystem, not a personality cult. |

## 4. Reference Accounts for Competitive Intelligence

| Account | Why Study Them |
|---------|----------------|
| **@godsays_live** | "App design and marketing is perfect." This account represents the gold standard for faith-app marketing on social media. Study their visual aesthetic, tonal choices, and engagement patterns to understand what resonates with the devout Christian audience. |
| **@god_of_spirit** | Reference for content style and visual approach. Their content bridges the gap between "inspirational" and "scriptural" — a balance ETHOS needs to strike in its own marketing. |

### What to Study

For each reference account, document the following in a competitive intelligence brief:

| Dimension | What to Capture |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Posting cadence** | How often do they post? What days and times perform best? Is there a pattern (e.g., daily morning posts, 3x/week Reels)? |
| **Visual style** | Color palette, typography, image vs. text ratio, use of video vs. static posts, story aesthetic. How does their visual identity convey "faith" without being cheesy? |
| **Caption format** | Length, tone (conversational vs. formal), use of Scripture, call-to-action style, hashtag strategy. |
| **Engagement rate** | Likes, comments, shares, saves per post. Which content types drive the highest engagement? |
| **Follower demographics** | Age range, gender split, geographic distribution (use third-party tools if needed). Does their audience overlap with ETHOS's ICP? |
| **Content themes** | What topics recur? Daily devotionals, life advice, prayer prompts, testimony stories? Which themes get the most engagement? |
| **App download strategy** | How do they drive users from social media to their app? Link in bio, story swipe-ups, pinned posts, specific CTAs? What is the conversion funnel from "follower" to "app user"? |

### Actionable Output

After studying these accounts, produce a **1-page brief** for each with:

1. Top 3 tactics ETHOS should adopt (with specific examples)
2. Top 3 mistakes to avoid (things they do that wouldn't work for ETHOS)
3. Estimated audience overlap with ETHOS ICP (high / medium / low)

## 5. Partnership Outreach Templates

### Template A — Cold Creator Outreach

Use this template for initial contact with creators on the roster (Section 2) or newly identified creators. The goal is a low-pressure introduction that leads to a 15-minute exploratory call.

---

**Subject:** Collab on something that actually helps people grow in faith?

Hey [Name],

I've been following your content on [specific post/topic] and it resonates with what we're building at ETHOS — a biblical companion app that gives personalized Scripture and guidance for the moments that matter.

We're looking for faith creators who walk the walk to help us reach young Christians who are serious about their growth. Not a generic sponsorship — we want your voice shaping how this reaches people.

Would you be open to a 15-min call to explore? No pressure, no commitment.

[Name]
ETHOS — For the person you're becoming.

---

**Template A Usage Notes:**

- **Always reference a specific piece of their content.** "I've been following your content" is the bare minimum. Better: "Your post about [specific topic] on [date] caught my attention because [specific reason]." This proves you've actually engaged with their work and aren't mass-blasting DMs.
- **Send via DM first, not email.** Creators live in their DMs. Email feels formal and corporate — the opposite of the vibe ETHOS wants to project.
- **Follow up once after 5 days** if no response. After that, move on. Chasing creators signals desperation.
- **Never offer money in the first message.** The goal is authentic partnership, not a transaction. If the first question a creator asks is "how much?", they're probably not the right fit.

---

### Template B — Founding Leader Pitch

Use this template for recruiting Founding Leaders (Section 3). This is a higher-stakes outreach — the goal is to secure a commitment from a proven leader to join the platform at launch.

---

**Subject:** Invitation to be a Founding Leader on ETHOS

[Name],

Your work in [their domain] has impacted thousands. We're building something that lets you mentor the next generation at scale — without the overhead.

ETHOS is launching a Leaders feature where proven Christian entrepreneurs can offer mentorship to young Christians navigating faith, career, and identity. As a Founding Leader, you'd get:

- 70/30 revenue split (in your favor)
- Zero upfront cost — we handle tech, payments, moderation
- 100% ownership of your content
- Equity options as a founding contributor

We're inviting 15 leaders to launch with. Your name came up because [specific reason].

15 minutes to explore?

[Name]
ETHOS — For the person you're becoming.

---

**Template B Usage Notes:**

- **This should come from Imman or a co-founder, not a marketing coordinator.** The recipient needs to feel that this is a peer-to-peer invitation, not a cold sales email.
- **The "[specific reason]" line is critical.** Generic flattery ("your amazing work") fails. Specific reference ("your talk at [conference] about [topic] aligned perfectly with our mission because [reason]") works.
- **Attach a 1-page brief** (not a 20-slide deck) that covers: What ETHOS is, what the Leaders feature looks like, the revenue model, and the founding leader terms. Keep it visual — screenshots of the app, a simple revenue share breakdown, a timeline.
- **Be transparent about scale.** Don't pretend ETHOS has millions of users. Leaders respect honesty: "We're early. We have [X] engaged users. We're inviting you to help build this from the ground up." The equity offer reinforces this — they're joining early because the upside is real.
- **The ask is always "15 minutes."** Not "join our platform." Not "sign this contract." Fifteen minutes to explore. Low commitment, high curiosity. The pitch does its job in the call, not in the email.

### Template C — Follow-Up After No Response

Use this 5-7 days after sending Template A or B with no response.

---

Hey [Name],

Just bumping this in case it got buried. Totally understand if the timing isn't right — but wanted to make sure you saw it.

Quick version: We're building ETHOS, a biblical companion app for young Christians. Looking for authentic voices like yours to help shape how it reaches people. 15 minutes, no strings.

Either way, keep doing what you're doing. Your content matters.

[Name]

---

**Follow-up rules:**

- **One follow-up only.** If they don't respond to the follow-up, respect the silence.
- **Never follow up more than once.** Three messages with no response is harassment, not persistence.
- **If they respond with "not right now"**, thank them and add them to a 90-day re-engagement list. Circle back in 3 months with a progress update.
