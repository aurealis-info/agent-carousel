# 06 — Content Strategy & Social Media Playbook

This document defines the content strategy, social media playbook, and production workflow for ETHOS. Every piece of content serves one purpose: bring young Christian men (18-25) closer to the app by meeting them where they actually are — in their struggle, in their ambition, and in the gap between the man they are and the man God intended them to be.

The thesis verse is James 4:8: "Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you." Every post, reel, carousel, and story should feel like that verse in action — a direct call to close the distance between a man and his Creator.

> **Two layers operate here.** The four **Pillars** below define *the kind of content beat* (Scripture Applied / The Grind / Real Talk / The Vision). The ten **Themes** in [`../guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES`](../guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES) define *the substance the beat is about* (governed · kills what's killing him · works · tells truth · strong AND tender · sacrificial leadership · planted in real men · chases God not wife · fights phone · walks humbly). Every piece sits at one pillar × one or two themes. The pillar/theme matrix at the end of this section makes the cross product explicit.

---

## 1. Content Pillars

All ETHOS content maps to four thematic pillars. Every post must belong to one. This prevents content drift and keeps the brand locked into its identity: dark, masculine, Scripture-backed, no fluff.

### Pillar 1: Scripture Applied

**Purpose:** Mirror the Daily Anchor feature. Show that Scripture is not a relic — it speaks directly into the situations a young man faces right now. The job interview. The argument with his girlfriend. The 2 AM temptation. The anger he does not know what to do with.

**Format:** Verse + practical application + "what this looks like in a man's real life."

**Examples:**
- "James 1:19 — 'Be quick to listen, slow to speak.' What that looks like when she pushes every button you have."
- "Proverbs 16:9 says your plans are in God's hands. Here's what that means when you don't get the job."
- "Philippians 4:6 isn't about pretending anxiety doesn't exist. It's about what a man does with it."
- "1 Corinthians 16:13 — 'Be on your guard; stand firm in the faith; be courageous; be strong.' That's not a suggestion."

**Tone:** Direct, grounded, practical. The reader finishes and thinks, "I can use this today." No fluff. No theological abstraction. Scripture applied to a man's Tuesday.

**Frequency:** 2x per week minimum across formats.

---

### Pillar 2: The Grind

**Purpose:** Discipline, consistency, and the daily fight of showing up. This pillar speaks to the reality that becoming the man God intended is not a one-time decision — it is a daily war. Every morning you wake up and choose the path again. This pillar honors that fight.

**Guiding Principle:** "I almost gave in" always outperforms "5 tips for success." Raw honesty about the grind — the alarm at 5 AM, the temptation at midnight, the discipline to stay consistent when nobody is watching — resonates far more than polished advice.

**Examples:**
- "Nobody clapped when you opened Scripture instead of Instagram this morning. God noticed."
- "The hardest part of following God isn't the big decisions. It's Tuesday at 3 PM when nobody's watching."
- "You don't become a man of God in a moment. You become one in a thousand small choices nobody sees."
- "Discipline isn't punishment. It's the bridge between the man you are and the man you're supposed to be."

**Tone:** Gritty, honest, motivational without being soft. Think locker room meets prayer closet. The arc is always: struggle --> discipline --> faithfulness.

**Frequency:** 2x per week.

---

### Pillar 3: Real Talk

**Purpose:** The struggles men do not talk about at church. Lust. Anger. Doubt. Pride. Loneliness. Pornography. The stuff that eats a man alive in silence because he thinks he is the only one. This pillar breaks that silence with Scripture and honesty.

**Guiding Principle:** Shame thrives in secrecy. When a man sees his exact struggle named — openly, without judgment, with a Bible verse attached — it strips the shame of its power. This is the pillar that builds the deepest trust and the strongest emotional connection to the brand.

**Examples:**
- "You opened the app at 2 AM because you were tempted and had nowhere else to go. You're not alone."
- "Every man in your small group is fighting something he hasn't told you about. You probably are too."
- "Lust doesn't make you disqualified. It makes you human. What you do next makes you a man of God."
- "Pride is the sin that convinces you that you don't have any sins. Check yourself."
- "You can't white-knuckle your way out of temptation. You need God's strength, not your own."

**Tone:** Confessional, direct, redemptive. Never preachy. Never shaming. The arc is always: honest struggle --> Scripture's answer --> the path forward for a man who wants to be better.

**Frequency:** 1-2x per week.

---

### Pillar 4: The Vision

**Purpose:** Who you are becoming. This pillar paints the picture of the destination — leadership, fatherhood, marriage, purpose, legacy. It answers the question: "Why am I doing this?" Because you are not just reading a Bible verse. You are building the man who leads his family, stays faithful to his wife, raises his sons with intention, and leaves his community better than he found it.

**Examples:**
- "Your future wife is praying for a man who fears God. Are you becoming that man?"
- "Your kids won't remember what you said. They'll remember who you were. Start building that man now."
- "Leadership isn't a title. It's showing up when it's hard, telling the truth when it costs you, and putting others first when nobody's watching."
- "Every verse you read, every prayer you pray, every temptation you resist — it's all building someone. Make sure it's the right someone."
- "The world doesn't need more successful men. It needs more faithful ones."

**Tone:** Aspirational, visionary, heavy. This pillar should make a man sit up straighter. It connects the daily grind to the eternal purpose. The arc is: today's discipline --> tomorrow's man --> God's design for your life.

**Frequency:** 1x per week minimum.

### Pillar × Theme Matrix

The four pillars (above) and the ten themes (in [`GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES`](../guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES)) are orthogonal. Pillars are *how the beat lands* (verse + practical application, daily-grind honesty, the unconfessed struggle, the long vision). Themes are *what the beat is about* (the substantive ground of righteous manhood). A given post sits at a single intersection.

| Theme | Natural Pillar Fit | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — He is governed | Scripture Applied · The Grind | Self-mastery is daily and Scripture-shaped. |
| 2 — He kills what's killing him | Real Talk · The Grind | The unspoken sin (porn, the phone) named, then put to death. |
| 3 — He works | The Grind · The Vision | Daily faithfulness; building the man worth marrying. |
| 4 — He tells the truth | Real Talk · The Vision | Integrity across contexts; confession as healing. |
| 5 — He's strong AND tender | The Vision · Real Talk | The both-and that breaks both feral and domesticated framings. |
| 6 — He leads by giving himself away | The Vision · Scripture Applied | Sacrificial leadership grounded in Ephesians 5. |
| 7 — He's planted in real men | The Vision · The Grind | Iron sharpens iron — and only with contact. |
| 8 — He chases God, not a wife | Scripture Applied · The Vision | Order matters; Matthew 6:33 in practice. |
| 9 — He fights the phone | The Grind · Real Talk | The 2026-specific battle, named honestly. |
| 10 — He walks humbly | Scripture Applied · The Vision | Micah 6:8; sturdy not soft. |

**Reading the matrix:** when planning a post, pick the pillar (the beat) first, then the theme (the substance). The pairing tells you the angle.

### Aspirational Bridge Topics

A category of topics that bridges biblical wisdom with worldly aspirations the audience already cares about — money, mornings, work, ambition, time, decisions, the body, brotherhood. These pull in viewers who weren't searching for "Christian content" but find themselves stopped by Scripture's answer to a question they were already asking.

These are **not** prosperity-gospel topics. The frame is always: *Scripture has wisdom about [worldly domain], and that wisdom contradicts what the internet is teaching you.* Wisdom-Applied (Format E) and Numbered Listicle (Format A) are the natural formats. Pillar fit is mostly **Scripture Applied** and **The Grind**, with occasional **The Vision** when the topic is about who he's becoming.

**Why this category exists.** The 20 "How a man of God…" topics above are *inward-focused* — character traits, struggles, the man under the hood. They serve the man already in the fight. Aspirational Bridge topics are *outward-focused* — the life that flows from that character. They recruit the man who's still figuring out he's in a fight at all. We need both.

**Topic areas in this category** (each generates many specific carousels — these are zones, not single posts):

- **A godly man + money** — stewardship, generosity, contentment vs. hustle / grindset / prosperity gospel.
- **A godly man's morning** — daily disciplines that shape the day before the day shapes him.
- **A godly man + work** — Proverbs / Ecclesiastes wisdom on ambition, talent, labor; what Solomon got right about hard work that the internet got wrong.
- **A godly man + decisions** — discernment over "follow your gut" / "trust the algorithm."
- **A godly man + time** — stewardship over optimization; rest as a discipline, not a reward.
- **A godly man + the body** — fitness and physical discipline as worship, not as identity.
- **A godly man + influence** — leading without becoming a personal-brand peddler.
- **A godly man + brotherhood** — the friendships that sharpen vs. the ones that flatter.
- **Faith + [worldly aspiration] crossovers** — pairing growth in faith with growth in something the audience aspires to (financial freedom, fitness, career, marriage, time mastery), framed so Scripture is the *deeper* answer the worldly version was reaching for.

**How to use this category.** When the editorial calendar feels too inward / too "struggle-coded," pull a topic from here. When you want to widen the funnel, pull from here. When you're trying to convert @stoicmindset0-coded audiences (already interested in masculine self-improvement, faith-curious or faith-adjacent), pull from here.

## 3. Content Formats

> For the substantive layer (themes, voice, audience reality), see [`marketing/guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES`](../guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES). For carousel-specific execution (intention, structure, hook engineering, copy psychology, batched production), see [`marketing/guidelines/CAROUSEL/guidelines.md`](../guidelines/CAROUSEL/guidelines.md). ### The Five Formats (Genre Reference)

Analysis of high-performing creators in the biblical-masculinity niche surfaced five distinct content formats. Each format pairs naturally with one or more pillars.

| Format | Description | Best pillar fit |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Numbered Listicle Carousel** | Cover → 1 principle per slide → verse → 3 verb-first bullets → aphorism. The `@lipolens` "10 Ways to be a Biblical Masculine Man" template. | Scripture Applied · The Grind · The Vision |
| **B — How-To Psychology Reel** | Behavior-design + Scripture. Habit-stacking, 2-minute rule, lower the bar. Atomic Habits meets the Bible. | The Grind · Scripture Applied |
| **C — Conviction Reel** | Direct address. Hard truth → Scripture-shaped reframe → diagnostic questions → ownership line. The "sit with that" format. | Real Talk · The Vision |
| **D — When-No-One's-Watching Reel** | List of ~4 hidden behaviors that signal character without performance. Anti-performance, observational. | The Vision · The Grind |
| **E — Wisdom-Applied Reel** | A worldly success topic (work, money, leadership) reframed as Proverbs / Ecclesiastes wisdom. Solomon-coded. | Scripture Applied · The Grind |

Mix formats across the week. Same pillar shipped in two different formats keeps the feed fresh without changing the underlying message.

### Instagram Carousels

Carousels are the primary format for depth, shareability, and saves. Every carousel follows the 5-slide formula:

**Slide 1 — Hook:**
- Dark-themed background (deep charcoal, matte black, or dark texture with subtle grain)
- 2-6 word hook text overlaid in bold, white or gold typography
- Must stop the scroll in under 1 second
- Never mention ETHOS or any product reference
- Examples: "The sin you won't admit" / "What a man of God does at 2 AM" / "5 Proverbs before you turn 25"

**Slide 2 — Value Tip #1:**
- First insight, story beat, or Scripture application
- Clean layout, readable font, minimal text (under 40 words)
- Dark background maintained throughout
- No product reference

**Slide 3 — Value Tip #2:**
- Second insight or deeper layer
- Build emotional momentum
- No product reference

**Slide 4 — App Tie-In:**
- Transition from content to product naturally
- Example: "That's why ETHOS exists. One verse. Matched to what you're actually facing. Every day."
- Subtle introduction, not a hard sell

**Slide 5 — CTA + App Screenshot:**
- Clear call to action: "Download ETHOS — link in bio"
- App screenshot showing the feature referenced in slide 4 (dark UI)
- ETHOS logo in branded footer

**Design Rules:**
- Create 3 design variations per carousel (different text treatments, background textures, accent color intensity) for A/B testing
- Dark backgrounds are non-negotiable. Black, charcoal, deep navy. Gold accents for emphasis.
- Bold typography — no thin, delicate fonts. This brand has weight.
- Use the ig-carousel-creator skill for production

---

### Reels

Reels are for emotional impact, app demonstration, and reaching new audiences through the algorithm. Every reel should have masculine energy — direct, confident, purposeful.

**Format 1 — Real Struggle Walkthrough:**
- Opens with a real, specific struggle spoken aloud or as text overlay: "I'm struggling with lust" / "I got angry and I don't know why" / "I feel like God isn't listening"
- Shows the user opening ETHOS on their phone
- Camera captures the app responding in real-time (check-in tag selection --> personalized verse)
- Closes on the user's reaction — resolve, not softness
- Duration: 15-30 seconds

**Format 2 — Build-in-Public with Imman:**
- Behind-the-scenes footage of Imman building ETHOS
- Direct-to-camera narration. No filler. "I built this because I needed it. Here's why."
- Shows the vision: who this app is for, what kind of man it's building
- Duration: 30-60 seconds

**Format 3 — Verse of the Day:**
- Dark, clean visual with a verse animated on screen
- Deep instrumental background (cinematic, not lo-fi worship)
- Bold typography, gold accents on dark background
- Ends with ETHOS branding
- Duration: 10-15 seconds

**Format 4 — Man of God Energy:**
- Short, punchy motivational clip with Scripture backing
- @stoicmindset0 energy — the kind of content men screenshot and send to each other
- Text overlay with hard-hitting truth, verse reference at the end
- Duration: 7-15 seconds

### Stories

Stories are for daily engagement, community interaction, and viral loop activation.

**Verse Shares from the App:**
- Users share their daily verse card directly from ETHOS to their Instagram story
- The share card is dark-branded with the ETHOS logo and a subtle "Draw near." CTA
- This is the primary viral loop mechanic — every share is organic distribution to a man's network of friends who are just like him

**Polls and Engagement:**
- Quick interactive polls: "What's harder — resisting temptation or admitting you failed?"
- "This or that" sliders: "Discipline or talent?" / "Prayer or action?"
- Question boxes: "What verse got you through this week?" / "What's your biggest struggle right now?"

**Behind-the-Scenes:**
- Raw updates from the build process
- "We just shipped this feature" screenshots
- Honest moments: "This is why I'm building this. For men like us."

### Static Posts

Static posts are for consistency, aesthetic cohesion, and easy sharing.

**Format:** Single image with verse text + one-line application on a branded template.

**Design:** Dark background (matte black, charcoal, deep navy). Gold or white bold typography. ETHOS watermark in corner. Clean, masculine, quotable.

**Purpose:** The "always-on" content that fills the grid, maintains cadence, and gives followers something strong and shareable. Think the kind of post a man saves to his camera roll.

## 5. Production Workflow

### Step 1: Topic Selection
- Pull from the content pillar rotation and monthly theme
- Reference the approved 20 topics and the 10 supplementary topics
- Ensure each week covers at least 2 different pillars

### Step 2: Content Creation
- **Carousels:** Use the ig-carousel-creator skill. Input: hook text, value points, CTA, and app screenshot. Output: 3 design variations for A/B testing. Dark theme, gold accents, bold type.
- **Reels:** Record raw footage (screen recordings, talking head, b-roll). Edit in CapCut or Premiere Rush. Add captions always. Cinematic/deep instrumentals for audio.
- **Static posts:** Design in the branded dark template. Swap verse and background texture per post.
- **Stories:** Create in-app or in Canva. Keep raw and authentic.

### Step 3: Image Sourcing
- **Pinterest** is the primary source for background imagery and mood references
- Search terms: "dark minimal aesthetic," "masculine texture background," "gold on black typography," "cinematic dark portrait," "man silhouette light"
- For Reels and behind-the-scenes: real footage only. No stock.
- Save to a shared Pinterest board for team access
- Always verify image licensing before use

### Step 4: Design Pipeline (Figma)
- All carousel and static post templates live in Figma
- Master template includes: slide dimensions, font styles, color swatches, logo placement
- Use **Google Stitch** (or equivalent) to generate 3 design variations per carousel slide
- Variations should differ in: background texture, gold accent intensity, font weight/style, text placement
- This enables rapid A/B testing without redesigning from scratch

### Step 5: Review Process
- All content goes through review before posting:
  1. **Theological accuracy check** — Is the Scripture referenced correctly? Is the application faithful to the text?
  2. **Brand voice check** — Does this sound like ETHOS (direct, masculine, Scripture-backed) or does it sound like a generic devotional account?
  3. **ICP alignment check** — Would a 21-year-old Christian man who follows @stoicmindset0 resonate with this? Would he save it? Would he send it to his boys?
  4. **Visual quality check** — Is the design dark, clean, and on-brand? Bold type? Gold accents? No pastels?
- Minimum 24-hour buffer between creation and posting for fresh-eyes review

### Step 6: Scheduling
- Use Later, Buffer, or Meta Business Suite for scheduling
- Batch content creation: produce one full week of content in a single session
- Schedule posts for optimal engagement times (test and iterate, but start with: Monday 7 AM, Tuesday 12 PM, Thursday 7 AM, Friday 6 PM, all in target audience's local time)

## 6. Imagery Guidelines for Content

### Do Use:
- **Dark, textured backgrounds** — matte black, charcoal, deep navy, dark concrete, brushed metal
- **Gold accents** — as highlight color for key words, verse numbers, divider lines, icons
- **Bold typography** — heavy weight, clean sans-serif or strong serif. Text that commands attention.
- **Cinematic portraits** — men in prayer, men in thought, silhouettes against light, strong posture
- **Subtle crosses** — integrated into dark compositions, not isolated or aggressive
- **Nature with edge** — mountains at dawn, storms, open roads, fire, stone. Creation that reflects strength.
- **Clean minimalism** — generous negative space on dark backgrounds. Let the words breathe.
- **Scripture on dark backgrounds** — verse text in gold or white on black/charcoal

### Do Not Use:
- **Warm, pastel, or light aesthetics** — cream, soft gold, sage, warm gray. This is not that brand.
- **Soft, devotional imagery** — candlelight, flowers, gentle sunsets. Signals a different audience.
- **Stock photos that feel staged** — forced smiles, corporate settings, unnatural lighting
- **Fitness or gym imagery** — signals self-improvement bro culture, not faith
- **Luxury or wealth imagery** — watches, cars, suits. Signals prosperity gospel.
- **Busy compositions** — too many elements, competing focal points, cluttered layouts
- **Generic "Christian app" aesthetics** — the warm/light look that every other faith app uses. ETHOS is intentionally different.

### Slide Balance Rule:
Alternate between text-heavy and image-heavy slides within every carousel. If slide 1 is image-dominant, slide 2 should be text-dominant, and so on. This maintains visual rhythm and prevents fatigue.

## 7. Tone & Voice Reference

### The ETHOS Voice

ETHOS speaks to men the way men actually talk to each other: direct, practical, no fluff. It is not a pastor at a pulpit. It is not a self-help guru. It is the older brother in faith who has been where you are, who tells you the truth even when it is uncomfortable, and who points you to Scripture every single time.

**Voice characteristics:**
- **Direct.** Short sentences. No hedging. Say what you mean.
- **Honest.** Name the real struggle. Lust, anger, doubt, pride — call it what it is.
- **Scripture-backed.** Every claim, every challenge, every encouragement ties back to the Word.
- **Aspirational.** Always point toward the man God intended. Never let him stay comfortable.
- **Quotable.** If a man wouldn't screenshot it and send it to his friend, it is not punchy enough.

**What ETHOS sounds like:**
- "You know the man you're supposed to be. Stop making excuses and start becoming him."
- "God didn't call you to be comfortable. He called you to be faithful."
- "One verse. Every day. Matched to what you're actually going through. That's the Daily Anchor."
- "Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you. James 4:8. That's the whole thesis."

**What ETHOS does NOT sound like:**
- "We're here for you on this journey of self-discovery and spiritual exploration."
- "Take a deep breath and remember that God loves you just as you are."
- "Today's gentle reminder: you are enough."
