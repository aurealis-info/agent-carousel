# 03 — Visual Identity & Design Direction

> This document defines ETHOS's visual identity for its core audience: young Christian men aged 18-25 who are building discipline, conviction, and faith. Every design decision must serve the mission: help men become who God intended them to be. The aesthetic is dark, masculine, aspirational, and Scripture-grounded. Think @stoicmindset0 meets the Word of God.

---

## 1. Design Direction

The April 4, 2026 design PDF (`ETHOSdesign_April4.pdf`) establishes the visual language that defines ETHOS going forward. The dark, bold, masculine aesthetic is not a mismatch with the audience — it IS the audience.

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Background** | Deep black / near-black throughout all screens |
| **Accent color** | Gold / amber highlights — used for CTAs, active states, emphasis, and Scripture references |
| **Typography** | Serif italic for Scripture display; bold sans-serif for UI text and headings |
| **Navigation** | 4-tab bottom nav: Chat, Bible, Home, Profile |
| **Data visualization** | Radar chart (spider graph) for growth tracking — rendered in gold on black |
| **Imagery style** | Dark, high-contrast, cinematic — moody lighting, silhouettes, dramatic landscapes |
| **Overall feel** | A daily operating system for masculine spiritual growth — not a devotional app, not a meditation app |

### Why This Design Works

- **The ICP is young men (18-25).** Dark, bold, high-contrast design is what they respond to. It looks like something they would actually have on their phone — not something their grandmother would download.
- **Gold/amber on black communicates premium authority.** It reads as serious, aspirational, and set apart — exactly how Scripture should feel when it lands in a moment of struggle.
- **Serif italic for Scripture creates reverence.** God's Word is visually distinct from everything else in the app. The typographic hierarchy mirrors the spiritual hierarchy: Scripture above all.
- **The radar chart is a shareable, ownable asset.** Gold lines on a dark field. Visually distinctive. Instagram-ready. A 20-year-old man will share this. He will not share a pastel card with a sunset.
- **The 4-tab navigation is purposeful and clean.** No clutter. No feature bloat. A tool, not a toy.

### The Guiding Principle

> ETHOS should feel like a locker room pep talk grounded in Scripture. Not a spa with gospel music.

The design must speak to men the way men actually talk to each other: direct, practical, no fluff. The aesthetic should make a man feel like he is picking up a weapon for the day — his verse, his prayer, his accountability — not browsing a content library.

## 2. Design-ICP Alignment

The dark, masculine visual language is aligned with the target audience. This section explains why.

### Who the ICP Is

Young Christian men, 18-25. College students, early-career men, athletes, military, tradesmen, young fathers. They are navigating lust, doubt, anxiety, directionlessness, and the pressure to conform to a culture that does not share their values. They want to be leaders, good fathers, faithful husbands, men of conviction. They need a tool that meets them where they are — not where a church marketing team thinks they should be.

### What They Respond To

| Visual Signal | Why It Works for This ICP |
|---------------|--------------------------|
| **Dark backgrounds** | Signals seriousness, depth, and focus. This is not a casual app. It is a daily discipline. |
| **Gold/amber accents** | Premium, aspirational, and connected to divine light in Christian tradition. Gold communicates "this matters." |
| **Bold typography** | Reads as confident and direct. The app does not whisper — it speaks with conviction. |
| **High contrast** | Visually arresting. Cuts through the noise of a 20-year-old's phone. Demands attention. |
| **Cinematic imagery** | Evokes the @stoicmindset0 aesthetic that already resonates with millions of young men — but grounded in Scripture, not Marcus Aurelius. |
| **Minimal, clean layouts** | Respects the user's time. No visual noise. A man opens this app, checks in, gets his verse, and goes to war with his day. |

### What They Do NOT Respond To

| Visual Signal | Why It Fails for This ICP |
|---------------|--------------------------|
| **Pastel colors** | Reads as soft, feminine, or generic. A 20-year-old man will not put this on his home screen. |
| **Warm cream backgrounds** | Codes as "cozy devotional" — not the energy of daily accountability and masculine growth. |
| **Stock photos of groups holding hands** | Generic church marketing. Feels institutional, not personal. |
| **Soft, luminous lighting** | Reads as meditation app or women's wellness brand. |
| **Floral or nature-soft imagery** | Fields of flowers and gentle sunsets do not speak to a man fighting lust at 2 AM. |

### The Core Insight

> The design was built for the audience. Dark, bold, masculine, and aspirational IS what young Christian men respond to — because no other faith app is speaking to them like this. Every competitor looks like it was designed for "everyone," which means it was designed for no one in particular. ETHOS is designed for HIM.

## 3. Color Palette

### Primary Palette

| Color | Hex (Approximate) | Role | Psychology |
|-------|-------------------|------|------------|
| **Deep Black** | `#0A0A0A` | Primary background across all screens | Depth, focus, seriousness, premium quality — removes visual noise and makes content the hero |
| **Gold / Amber** | `#C8973E` | Primary accent, CTAs, active states, Scripture references, highlights | Divine light, premium quality, aspiration, warmth within darkness — the beacon in the black |
| **Charcoal** | `#1A1A1A` | Card surfaces, elevated elements, secondary backgrounds | Subtle depth layering without breaking the dark palette; creates visual hierarchy |
| **Warm White** | `#F0E6D6` | Primary text on dark surfaces | Readable, warm-toned white that avoids the harshness of pure white; feels intentional |
| **Steel Gray** | `#6B6B6B` | Secondary text, metadata, inactive states | Functional hierarchy; communicates "less important" without disappearing |

### Extended Palette

| Color | Hex (Approximate) | Role |
|-------|-------------------|------|
| **Burnished Gold** | `#A67C2E` | Darker gold variant for depth on hover/pressed states |
| **Dark Amber** | `#8B6914` | Tertiary accent for subtle UI elements |
| **Slate** | `#2A2A2E` | Alternate card surface for visual variety |
| **Off-Black** | `#111114` | Alternate background for section differentiation |
| **Ember** | `#C4501A` | Urgent or alert states — used sparingly for warnings, streak-at-risk indicators |

### Color Psychology Rationale

- **Deep black as the primary background** is non-negotiable. It communicates that this app is serious. It is a daily operating system, not a pastime. Black removes distraction and puts the focus entirely on the content: the verse, the check-in, the growth data. Every major app that young men gravitate toward (fitness, finance, gaming) uses dark interfaces. ETHOS speaks their visual language.
- **Gold/amber is the spiritual anchor.** In Christian tradition, gold represents divine glory, holiness, and the presence of God. In design, it communicates premium quality and aspiration. When a verse appears in gold on a black field, it reads as sacred and set apart. This is intentional. Scripture should look different from everything else on the user's phone.
- **Pure white is avoided for backgrounds.** White backgrounds code as generic, clinical, or soft. The warm white (`#F0E6D6`) used for text on dark surfaces carries warmth without sacrificing the dark aesthetic.
- **The palette has no pastels, no soft gradients, no warm cream.** These visual signals belong to a different product for a different audience. ETHOS is built for men who want to be held accountable, not comforted into complacency.

## 4. Typography Direction

### Scripture Display

| Property | Specification |
|----------|---------------|
| **Typeface** | Serif italic (retained and elevated as the sacred voice of the app) |
| **Weight** | Regular to Medium — authoritative without being heavy |
| **Size** | Generous — Scripture is always the largest text element on any screen where it appears |
| **Color** | Warm White on dark backgrounds; Gold for Scripture references |
| **Candidates** | EB Garamond Italic, Cormorant Garamond Italic, Playfair Display Italic |

**Rationale:** Serif italic for Scripture creates a visual distinction that mirrors the spiritual distinction. God's Word is set apart. It does not look like a push notification or a UI label. When a man sees the serif italic, he knows: this is the verse. This is the anchor. This is what he came for.

### UI / Headings / CTAs

| Property | Specification |
|----------|---------------|
| **Typeface** | Bold sans-serif — strong, readable, masculine |
| **Weight range** | Medium (body), SemiBold (labels), Bold (headings), ExtraBold (primary CTAs) |
| **Size** | 16px base minimum on mobile |
| **Candidates** | Inter, SF Pro, Barlow, Oswald (for display headings) |

**Rationale:** Bold sans-serif communicates directness and confidence. This is not a whispered invitation — it is a clear statement. Headings should feel like they are speaking TO the user. "Check In." "Your Verse." "Your Growth." Short. Bold. Direct.

### Typographic Hierarchy

```
Level 1: Screen titles         — Sans-serif, Bold, 24-28px, Warm White
Level 2: Section headers       — Sans-serif, SemiBold, 18-20px, Warm White
Level 3: Scripture reference   — Serif, Regular, 14-16px, Gold
Level 4: Scripture text        — Serif Italic, Medium, 20-24px, Warm White
Level 5: Body / explanation    — Sans-serif, Regular, 16px, Warm White
Level 6: Tags / metadata       — Sans-serif, Regular, 12-14px, Steel Gray
Level 7: CTAs                 — Sans-serif, Bold, 16-18px, Deep Black on Gold button
```

## 5. Imagery Guidelines

### The Aesthetic: @stoicmindset0 Meets Scripture

The imagery direction borrows the visual energy of accounts like @stoicmindset0 — dark, cinematic, masculine, aspirational — and grounds it in Christian faith. The result is imagery that a 20-year-old man stops scrolling for AND that points him toward God, not just toward self-improvement.

### DO Use

| Category | Examples | Why It Works |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Silhouettes of men** | A man standing on a mountain ridge at dawn, a man kneeling in prayer, a man walking alone on an empty road | Evokes strength, solitude with God, the individual walk of faith — the user sees himself |
| **Crosses in dramatic light** | A cross silhouetted against a storm sky, a cross at golden hour on a hilltop, a cross lit from behind | The most powerful symbol in Christianity, rendered with cinematic weight — not clip art |
| **Men in prayer / contemplation** | Hands clasped, head bowed, a man with an open Bible in a dark room lit by a single lamp | Real, masculine, intimate — shows that prayer is not passive but powerful |
| **Mountain ridges at dawn** | Dark foreground, golden light breaking on the horizon, vast and epic | Scale communicates the greatness of God; dawn communicates new mercies; darkness-to-light mirrors the daily check-in arc |
| **Moody / cinematic landscapes** | Storm clouds over open terrain, single trees on empty plains, dramatic skies | Creates emotional weight; says "the world is hard, but you are not alone in it" |
| **Warrior / armor imagery** | Ephesians 6 visual references — shields, swords, armor in dramatic light (abstract, not literal cosplay) | "Put on the full armor of God" is one of the most resonant passages for young men; visual references reinforce the daily preparation metaphor |
| **Dark rooms with single light sources** | Candlelight, a lamp on a Bible, light through a window onto a man reading | Intimacy, focus, the quiet discipline of daily time with God — cinematic and shareable |

### DO NOT Use

| Category | Examples | Why It Fails |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Soft / feminine imagery** | Flower fields, pastel gradients, watercolor textures, soft bokeh with warm tones | Codes as women's wellness or generic devotional; a 20-year-old man will swipe past this instantly |
| **Pastel colors in any context** | Light pink, lavender, mint green, baby blue in backgrounds or overlays | Alienates the ICP; contradicts the dark, bold brand positioning |
| **Generic "church" imagery** | Pews, hymnals, church lobbies, greeters shaking hands, potluck dinners | Institutional, not personal; ETHOS is a daily tool for the individual man, not a church marketing campaign |
| **Stock photos of diverse groups** | Groups of people holding hands in a circle, multicultural stock-photo smiles, posed group prayer | Reads as corporate or institutional; ETHOS speaks to the individual man's walk, not a group experience |
| **Irreverent religious imagery** | Religious figures in meme contexts, saints in secular settings, anything that treats sacred symbols as content props | Destroys trust with men who take their faith seriously |
| **Luxury / wealth signaling** | Sports cars, designer watches, mansion interiors | Codes as prosperity gospel or materialism; the ICP values conviction, not consumption |
| **Shirtless athletes / bodybuilder aesthetic** | Physique-focused imagery, gym mirrors, fitness transformation photos | Crosses the line from masculine aspiration into vanity; ETHOS builds the inner man, not the outer one |

### Imagery Composition Rules

1. **Dark-dominant.** Every image should have a predominantly dark palette. If the image would look at home on a pastel Pinterest board, it does not belong in ETHOS.
2. **Single subject or focal point.** One man. One cross. One horizon line. Simplicity communicates strength.
3. **Cinematic aspect ratios and lighting.** Wide shots for landscapes. Close crops for prayer/contemplation. Always moody, never flat.
4. **Text overlays must be minimal and high-contrast.** Gold or warm white text on dark imagery. Never more than one line of text over an image.
5. **Alternate between image-heavy and text-heavy content.** Let the visuals breathe on image slides. Let the words command attention on text slides.

### Template Design Principles

1. **Dark backgrounds only.** Every share template uses a deep black or near-black background. When this card appears on someone's Instagram story feed, it should stand out because of its boldness, not blend in with pastel devotionals.
2. **Gold is the signature color.** Scripture references in gold. Rules and dividers in gold. The ETHOS tagline in gold. Gold on black is the visual signature that makes ETHOS cards instantly recognizable.
3. **Typography hierarchy is non-negotiable.** Scripture text is the largest element. The reference is always in gold. The tagline is always the smallest element. The verse is the hero.
4. **No imagery on verse cards.** The words are the weapon. Let them stand alone on the dark field. The simplicity IS the power. Background can have very subtle texture (brushed metal, concrete, matte grain) but never a photograph competing with the verse.
5. **Snapshot cards are data-forward.** The radar chart is the hero. Gold lines on black. Clean, legible, and visually striking. A man should look at this and think: "I am making progress."
6. **Every card should pass the "home screen test."** Would a 20-year-old man save this to his camera roll? Would he post it? If the answer is no, redesign it.

## 7. Social Content Visual Direction

### Instagram Grid Aesthetic

The ETHOS Instagram grid should look like a single, cohesive visual experience — not a random collection of posts. When a potential user lands on the profile, the overall impression should be: dark, bold, masculine, Scripture-grounded, aspirational.

| Post Type | Visual Treatment |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Verse cards** | Black background, gold Scripture reference, warm white verse text, serif italic |
| **Conviction statements** | Black background, bold sans-serif white text, gold accent word or phrase |
| **Imagery posts** | Dark, cinematic photography — silhouettes, crosses, mountain ridges, moody lighting |
| **Growth data / radar** | Gold chart lines on black, clean data visualization |
| **Carousel openers** | Bold text hook on black — "Most men never..." / "The difference between..." |

### Grid Rules

1. No post should have a light or white background. The grid reads as a wall of dark, serious, intentional content.
2. Gold should appear in every third post minimum — maintaining the signature color throughout the scroll.
3. Alternate between text-heavy and image-heavy posts. Two text posts max in a row. Two image posts max in a row.
4. Every carousel ends with a Scripture reference on the final slide.
5. Reels thumbnails should use the same dark aesthetic — custom thumbnails, not auto-generated frames.
