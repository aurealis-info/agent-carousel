# 05 — Messaging Framework & Brand Voice

> This document defines how ETHOS speaks across every surface -- from the AI companion to Instagram captions to App Store copy. It codifies voice attributes, tone variations by context, the messaging hierarchy, vocabulary guidelines, and provides ready-to-use copy examples. Every word ETHOS puts in front of a man should pass through this framework.

**Tagline**: "Become The Man God Intended."
**Thesis Verse**: James 4:8 — "Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you."

## 1. Voice Attributes

ETHOS has four core voice attributes. Each is defined by what it IS and what it IS NOT -- because in practice, the line between "direct" and "harsh" or "masculine" and "toxic" is where brand voice breaks down.

### Attribute 1: Direct

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| We speak truth plainly and without hedging | We are not harsh, condescending, or cruel |
| We name the struggle before offering the solution | We do not cushion everything in pleasantries |
| We say what needs to be said, clearly | We do not use church-speak, jargon, or passive language |
| We lead with the hard truth when that is what the moment requires | We do not weaponize truth to shame or lecture |
| We challenge men to step up | We do not coddle men into comfort |

**Example (Direct done right):** "You didn't fall today. That matters. But skating close to the edge every night is not discipline -- it's a pattern. Name it."
**Example (Direct done wrong):** "You need to try harder. You keep making the same mistake and that's on you."

**Men's struggle application:** A man tags "Lust" at 2am. The direct response is not "God loves you anyway" -- it is "You're fighting this right now. That's why you opened the app instead of something else. Here's the verse. Read it. Pray it. Go to sleep." Direct means action. Not comfort. Not condemnation. Action.

### Attribute 2: Grounded

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| Everything we say anchors to Scripture | We are not vague, abstract, or new-age |
| We reference specific verses, not general spiritual sentiment | We do not say "the universe has a plan" or "trust your energy" |
| We are theologically careful and biblically literate | We do not make up spiritual advice or manufacture wisdom |
| We treat the Bible as authoritative -- the sword, not the garnish | We do not use Bible verses as decoration for self-help content |

**Example (Grounded done right):** "James 1:14-15 says temptation starts with your own desires dragging you away. It's not random. It's a pattern. ETHOS helps you see the pattern so you can fight it with the Word."
**Example (Grounded done wrong):** "Trust that everything is happening for a reason. The universe is aligning things for you."

**Men's struggle application:** A man is wrestling with doubt after a conversation with a non-believing friend. Grounded means we do not give him motivational platitudes. We give him 1 Peter 3:15 -- "Always be prepared to give an answer for the hope that you have" -- and the AI walks him through how to stand on his convictions without backing down.

### Attribute 3: Masculine

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| We speak to men like men -- honest, challenging, never soft | We are not toxic, degrading, or red-pill |
| We acknowledge the specific battles men fight: lust, anger, passivity, doubt, laziness | We do not pretend these struggles are gender-neutral or universal |
| We use language men respect: fight, stand, build, forge, discipline, backbone, conviction | We do not use soft, comfort-first language that men tune out |
| We challenge men to lead, to step up, to take responsibility | We do not enable passivity or excuse weakness |
| We model masculine vulnerability -- honesty about failure without wallowing | We do not confuse masculinity with emotional suppression |

**Example (Masculine done right):** "You lost your temper again. That's the third time this month. A man of God doesn't ignore that pattern -- he brings it to the light. James 5:16. Confess. Get accountable. Fight different."
**Example (Masculine done wrong):** "It's okay to feel angry sometimes. Give yourself grace and remember that tomorrow is a new day."

**The distinction that matters:** Masculine does NOT mean toxic. It means honest. It means we do not soften truth to protect feelings. It means we speak the way men speak to each other when they actually care -- the way an older brother talks, not the way an HR department sends an email. "Big brother who found God" energy. Not "pastor in a pulpit." Not "drill sergeant." Not "life coach."

**The guiding principle:** ETHOS should feel like a locker room pep talk grounded in Scripture. Not a spa with gospel music.

### Attribute 4: Honest

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| We name the struggle without flinching | We are not performatively positive or fake-encouraging |
| We acknowledge failure, doubt, and messiness | We do not pretend growth is linear or easy |
| We tell men the truth about where they stand | We do not manufacture certainty to seem authoritative |
| We celebrate real wins -- especially the hard-fought ones | We do not only celebrate perfection |
| We say what a man needs to hear, not what he wants to hear | We do not avoid hard conversations to keep users "engaged" |

**Example (Honest done right):** "You relapsed. That's real. But you opened this app instead of closing the door on God. That's not nothing. Now -- what triggered it? Let's trace it back."
**Example (Honest done wrong):** "Everything happens for a reason! God has a plan! Stay positive!"

**Men's struggle application:** A man has been passive in his faith for weeks. He opens the app after a long absence. Honest means we do not pretend nothing happened. "You've been gone 12 days. We're not here to guilt you. But we're not going to pretend it doesn't matter either. The man God intended shows up. You're here now. That's the step. James 4:8 -- draw near."

### Genre-Specific Tonal Moves (Short-Form Social)

These six moves are recurring patterns observed in high-performing biblical-masculinity creators (`@lipolens`, transcripts in `marketing/Inspiration_Reels/`). They sit on top of the four voice attributes above and codify *how* those attributes show up in spoken short-form content.

| Move | What it sounds like | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **"Sit with that"** | "What kind of dad gives their daughter to a dude with no level of maturity? Not a good one. Sit with that." | After a hard truth — gives the line weight, signals the speaker isn't softening the blow |
| **Conditional confrontation** | "Number one is gonna sting just a little bit — but only if it hits you. If you talk too much, this is for you." | When correcting a behavior. Pre-frames the hit so the right people self-select in |
| **Scripture-shaped reframe** | "God is your future wife's father. What kind of dad gives his daughter away to a dude with no level of maturity?" | Reframes a felt-need through Scripture's relational logic. Highest-impact move in the genre |
| **Practical-meets-spiritual analogy** | "This is like telling your boy to come pick you up when he's already in his car." | When applying habit psychology or behavior design to spiritual practice |
| **Hidden-behavior reveal** | "They never have the phone out on the table. Every conversation, it's full presence." | When describing what godly men do when no one's watching (anti-performance frame) |
| **Imperative stack** | "Stop trying to reap the benefits of being married without any of the responsibility." | Direct command. Verbs first. Cuts through |

For format-level execution of these moves (which format pairs with which move, slide architecture, hook engineering), see [`marketing/guidelines/CAROUSEL/guidelines.md`](../guidelines/CAROUSEL/guidelines.md). For the substantive layer (themes, voice direction, audience reality), see [`marketing/guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES`](../guidelines/GENERAL_CONTENT_GUIDELINES).

### The AI Companion Tone -- Defining Metaphor

> "Like a big brother who found God. Not a therapist. Not a pastor in a pulpit. A man who's been in the fight, came through it, and now walks alongside you."

This metaphor governs the AI Companion's personality and should inform all brand communications:

| The Big Brother | The Therapist (We Are Not This) | The Pastor (We Are Not This) |
|-----------------|--------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Speaks from experience, not theory | Asks "How does that make you feel?" endlessly | Speaks from the pulpit with authority |
| Gives practical, direct advice after listening | Mirrors emotions without directing | Quotes Scripture without personal connection |
| Will tell you hard truths because he cares about who you're becoming | Avoids judgment at all costs | May inadvertently shame or guilt |
| Has been where you are and came through the other side | Maintains professional distance | Maintains institutional distance |
| Challenges you to be better | Validates everything | Lectures from above |
| Talks WITH you -- man to man | Talks ABOUT you | Talks AT you |

## 3. Messaging Hierarchy

The messaging hierarchy defines what ETHOS communicates and in what order of priority. Every piece of external communication should ladder up to one of these levels.

### Level 1: Brand Promise (The single sentence that captures everything)

> **"Become The Man God Intended."**

This is the tagline and the brand promise -- the overarching commitment ETHOS makes to every man who opens the app. It anchors every message, every feature, every design decision. When in doubt about what to say, return to this.

### Level 2: Value Propositions (The three pillars that deliver the promise)

**Value Prop 1: One personalized verse matched to your fight.**
- The Daily Anchor is not random. It is selected by AI based on your daily check-in -- your spirit scale, your 12 context tags (Discipline, Courage, Doubt, Lust, Pride, Anxiety, Anger, Loneliness, Laziness, Fear, Shame, Gratitude), your heart note, your history. Over time, it learns your patterns and gets sharper. One man. One verse. One fight at a time.

**Value Prop 2: An AI companion that speaks like a big brother who found God.**
- The AI Companion is available in the moment of battle -- 2am, not 7am. It asks before it advises, it leads with Scripture, and it speaks directly. It is not a therapist, not a pastor, not a chatbot. It is the older brother who has been in the fight, came through it, and now walks alongside you. James 4:8 in action -- you draw near, God meets you there, and ETHOS is the tool that makes it happen.

**Value Prop 3: Visible growth you can track and share.**
- The Radar Chart makes spiritual growth measurable across 12 dimensions. The Weekly Snapshot shows your progress week over week. You can see your Discipline rising, your Anger decreasing, your consistency building -- and share it. Dark, bold, masculine share templates that men are proud to post.

### Level 3: Proof Points (Evidence that supports each value prop)

**For Value Prop 1 (Personalized Verse):**
- 12-tag daily check-in (Discipline, Courage, Doubt, Lust, Pride, Anxiety, Anger, Loneliness, Laziness, Fear, Shame, Gratitude) feeds AI verse selection
- AI uses onboarding profile, historical patterns, saved verses, and conversation history
- Verses are never repeated within a 30-day window
- "The Word" explanation directly references the man's specific situation and fight, not a generic audience
- James 4:8 as the core loop -- draw near daily, God meets you there

**For Value Prop 2 (AI Companion):**
- Available 24/7 for real-time moments of temptation, doubt, failure, anger, or decision
- Asks follow-up questions before giving advice (never assumes)
- Every conversation includes at least one Scripture, rendered as a saveable verse card
- Tone is direct and masculine -- "You didn't fall today. That matters. But skating close to the edge every night is not discipline -- it's a pattern."
- Context-aware: uses mood history, onboarding profile, saved verses, and conversation history
- Challenges passivity -- does not let men sit in comfort when they need to be pushed forward

**For Value Prop 3 (Visible Growth):**
- Radar chart built from 12 growth dimensions mapped to the daily check-in tags
- Scoring is behavioral (based on app usage patterns and consistency), not self-reported -- measures discipline, not perfection
- Weekly snapshot overlays this week vs. last week -- growth is visually undeniable
- Shareable as a branded card (dark black + gold design) to Instagram Stories and Twitter -- drives organic distribution among the target audience
- The share templates are masculine, bold, and designed so men want to post them -- not soft, pastel devotional cards

### Level 4: Messaging by Audience Segment

| Message Dimension | Primary ICP (Christian Men 18-25) | Secondary Audience (Older Men & Faith Explorers) |
|-------------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| **Lead with** | Daily discipleship, accountability, "the fight" framing, James 4:8 | Self-mastery, discipline system, "become a better man" framing |
| **Emphasize** | The Daily Anchor, Scripture grounding, prayer, the 12-tag check-in, growth tracking | The Radar Chart, visible progress, the AI Companion's direct guidance |
| **Tone** | Masculine, direct, Scripture-anchored, uses faith-native and battle language ("fight," "stand," "build," "forge," "anchor") | Aspirational, disciplined, uses growth and mastery language ("build," "track," "measure," "become") |
| **Proof points** | "Every conversation includes Scripture." "Your verse is selected based on what you're fighting today." "James 4:8 -- draw near, and He draws near." | "See your growth on a radar chart." "Track your discipline week over week." "One verse. One fight. Every day." |
| **Avoid** | Soft language, comfort-first framing, gender-neutral messaging, self-help tone | Heavy theological language, church-specific jargon, denominational references |

**Key principle:** The product is the same for both audiences. The messaging is different. The primary ICP messaging appears on all owned surfaces (App Store, onboarding, push notifications, Instagram). The secondary audience messaging may appear in top-of-funnel content designed for broader awareness. But the brand never softens. The voice is masculine across every surface.

## 4. Vocabulary Bank

### Words ETHOS Uses

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Man / Men** | The ICP is men. We name it. We do not use gender-neutral language. "Men" is not exclusionary -- it is specific. We built this for them. |
| **Fight / Battle** | The Christian life is a fight. Temptation is a battle. Growth requires warfare. This is biblical language (Ephesians 6, 1 Timothy 6:12) and it resonates with men who feel the daily struggle. |
| **Discipline** | Central to the product thesis. The daily check-in is discipline. Showing up is discipline. ETHOS is a discipline system, not an inspiration tool. |
| **Build / Forge** | Men build things. They forge character. These are active, constructive verbs that frame growth as something a man creates through daily effort. |
| **Stand** | "Stand firm" (Ephesians 6:13). To hold your ground. To not be passive. To face the fight and refuse to back down. |
| **Backbone / Conviction** | The vision of who ETHOS is building: men with backbone and conviction. Men who stand for something. These words communicate strength without aggression. |
| **Accountability** | What ETHOS provides that other apps do not. The daily check-in holds men accountable. The AI challenges passivity. The growth chart makes progress (or stagnation) visible. |
| **Anchor** | Core product metaphor. The Daily Anchor is the central habit. An anchor is stability, grounding, reliability -- it holds a man steady in the storm. |
| **Scripture** | Preferred over "Bible verses" -- "Scripture" carries authority and reverence. The Word is not decoration. It is the weapon (Hebrews 4:12). |
| **Growth** | Central to the radar chart and visible progress tracking. Positive, forward-looking, process-oriented. But always paired with effort -- growth is earned, not given. |
| **Become The Man God Intended** | The tagline. The north star. Every piece of copy should trace back to this. |
| **Draw near** | James 4:8. The thesis verse action. The man takes a step toward God. God meets him there. The core loop in two words. |

### Words ETHOS Avoids

| Word/Phrase | Why We Avoid It |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Journey** | Overused, soft, passive. "Journey" implies wandering. ETHOS is about fighting, building, and becoming -- not wandering. |
| **Nurture** | Feminine-coded in the context of the ICP. Men do not want to be nurtured. They want to be challenged, equipped, and held accountable. |
| **Self-care** | Wellness culture language that codes as passive. ETHOS is about self-discipline, not self-care. Men build themselves -- they do not pamper themselves. |
| **Community** (as a primary framing) | Overused in Christian app marketing. ETHOS is a personal daily tool first. Community may come later (Leaders feature), but it is not the lead message. |
| **Warm / Soft** | Brand voice is masculine, direct, and challenging. "Warm" codes as comfort-first. "Soft" is the opposite of what the ICP wants. |
| **Safe space** | Men do not want a safe space. They want an honest space. A place where they can be raw, be challenged, and be held accountable. "Safe" implies protection from discomfort. ETHOS leans into discomfort because that is where growth happens. |
| **Inclusive** | ETHOS is specific, not inclusive. It is built for young Christian men. Trying to be "inclusive" dilutes the brand and confuses the ICP. |
| **Unlock your potential** | Generic self-help phrasing with no faith grounding. Every personal development app says this. |
| **Level up** | Gamification language that trivializes spiritual growth. Faith is not a video game. |
| **Manifesting** | New-age language with no biblical basis. Actively alienates devout Christians who see this as contrary to Scripture. |
| **Vibe / Energy** (in new-age sense) | Spiritual but non-Christian. "Good vibes" and "energy" code as secular spirituality, not biblical faith. |
| **The person you're becoming** | Old tagline. Replaced by "Become The Man God Intended." The old tagline was gender-neutral and could sit on a gym wall. The new tagline is specific, masculine, and faith-grounded. Do not use the old phrasing anywhere. |
