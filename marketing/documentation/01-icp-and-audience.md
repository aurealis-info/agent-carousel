# 01 — Ideal Customer Profile & Audience

**Product**: ETHOS — "Become The Man God Intended"
**Category**: Daily discipleship app for young Christian men (18-25) pursuing masculine spiritual growth
**Thesis Verse**: James 4:8 — "Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you."
**Last updated**: 2026-04-04

---

## 1. The Governing Insight

ETHOS is not a Bible reading app. It is a daily operating system for masculine spiritual growth.

The men we serve are not looking for another devotional to skim. They are fighting real battles — lust, doubt, anger, laziness, anxiety — and they need a tool that meets them in the fight. Not after. Not on Sunday. Every single day.

The thesis is simple: if you give a young man one personalized verse matched to what he is actually facing, and pair it with AI-guided interpretation, daily prayer, and visible growth tracking — he will show up. And showing up daily is how men are built.

James 4:8: "Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you." That is the core loop. The man takes a step. God meets him there. ETHOS is the tool that makes the step concrete.

### The Audience Reality: Feral, Domesticated, or Governed

The 22-year-old Christian man we're building for is not abstractly "looking for spiritual growth." He's caught in a generational pinch between two failed versions of manhood — and the product only makes sense as the way out.

- **The feral version** of him — Tate-coded, hookup culture, dominate everything, "alpha," no master but his appetites. Looks like strength; reads like a slave with a loud mouth.
- **The domesticated version** of him — porn-fed, screen-fed, conflict-avoidant, soft spine, performative niceness. Looks like gentleness; reads like a man without walls.

ETHOS speaks to him as **the governed man** — under Christ, free in Galatians 5's sense (free to do what's right when it costs you), strong and tender in the right places, dangerous in the right direction. Not feral. Not domesticated. Free.

This is the load-bearing audience insight. He has heard the verses. He has been to church. He can quote half of Romans — and he still can't put the phone down at 1 AM. The problem is not information. **The problem is that he has never seen what a man looks like when Scripture has actually gotten its hands on him.** ETHOS exists to be the daily system that makes that man visible — and possible — to him.

### What We Are Building Toward

Men who are leaders, not followers. Fathers who are present. Husbands who are faithful. Men with backbone and conviction. The app is the daily tool that keeps them accountable to that vision of themselves.

### What ETHOS Is Not

- It is not a passive Bible reading tool. YouVersion already does that.
- It is not a soft, comfort-first devotional app. That market is saturated and it does not speak to men.
- It is not a self-improvement app with Scripture sprinkled in. The Bible is the foundation, not the garnish.
- It is not gender-neutral. The ICP is men. The voice is masculine. The design is intentional.

### Psychographics

**Growth orientation:**
- Ambitious, driven, or aspiring to be — even if they are not there yet
- Follow accounts like @stoicmindset0, masculine faith content, self-improvement creators who reference God
- Believe in discipline as a virtue, even when they struggle to maintain it
- See manhood as something you build, not something you fall into
- Want to be leaders — in their families, careers, communities, and churches

**Faith relationship:**
- Believe in God and want to live it out — not just claim it
- Read Scripture some days but lack a consistent daily system
- Pray, but often unstructured and inconsistent
- Attend church, or want to, but feel the gap between Sunday and the rest of the week
- Know the destination (Christlikeness) but lack the daily map

**Real struggles (the 12 check-in tags):**
- Discipline, Courage, Doubt, Lust, Pride, Anxiety, Anger, Loneliness, Laziness, Fear, Shame, Gratitude
- These are not abstract. These men fight lust at 2am. They battle doubt after a conversation with a non-believing friend. They carry anger they do not know how to process. They feel lazy and hate themselves for it.
- The struggles are real, recurring, and mostly unspoken in church contexts

**Content consumption:**
- Instagram: follows @stoicmindset0, masculine faith accounts, self-improvement creators, bodybuilding/fitness content with purpose framing
- YouTube: self-improvement content, faith-based teaching, workout routines, long-form interviews with men they admire
- TikTok: stoic/masculine content, faith clips, "become a better man" content
- Imman.faith: follows or is in the orbit of this audience — 150K+ followers, 18yo Christian male building in public
- Podcasts: mix of faith teaching and masculine development content

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Detail |
|---|---|
| No daily system for spiritual accountability | He knows he should be in the Word daily. He tries. He fails. He does not have a system that makes it automatic and personal. Willpower alone is not working. |
| Church gives the destination but not the daily map | Sunday sermon says "be a man of integrity" or "flee from temptation" — but Monday through Saturday is unguided. No one tells him what to do at 11pm when he is tempted. |
| Existing apps are passive | YouVersion gives a verse of the day but does not know his name, his struggle, or what he is carrying. It is a library, not a companion. Reading is not the same as being equipped. |
| No tool speaks to men the way men talk to each other | Christian apps default to soft, pastel, gender-neutral design and messaging. He does not see himself in them. He needs something direct, dark, masculine — something that challenges him instead of coddling him. |
| Real struggles go unspoken | Lust, porn, anger, doubt — these do not get talked about honestly in most church settings. He needs a private space to be raw about what he is actually facing, without judgment. |

### The Core Loop

This is what makes ETHOS different from every other faith app on the market:

```
CHECK IN → He selects from 12 tags (Discipline, Courage, Doubt, Lust, etc.)
               — honest, daily, takes 10 seconds

ONE VERSE → ETHOS delivers a single personalized Bible verse
               matched to what he is actually facing today

GO DEEPER → AI-guided interpretation and conversation
               — explains the verse, applies it to his situation, answers questions

PRAY     → Daily prayer grounded in his check-in and the verse
               — structured, personal, not generic

TRACK    → Growth tracking over time
               — makes spiritual progress visible and measurable
```

## 3. Secondary Audience: Older Christian Men & Men Exploring Faith

### Role in the Ecosystem

The primary ICP is 18-25, but ETHOS naturally extends to two adjacent groups:

| Segment | Age | Relationship to ETHOS |
|---|---|---|
| Older Christian men | 25-35 | Same struggles, higher stakes. Marriage, fatherhood, career leadership. They need the daily system too — and they have more spending power. |
| Men exploring faith | 18-30 | Not yet committed Christians, but drawn to the masculine + faith intersection. Self-improvement guys who feel the pull toward something greater. ETHOS can be the bridge. |

### How to Serve the Secondary Without Losing the Primary

1. **Do not age up the voice.** The brand speaks to 18-25. Older men who resonate will self-select in. If the voice shifts to 30+, the core ICP disconnects.
2. **Do not soften for seekers.** Men exploring faith are drawn to ETHOS because it is direct and bold. If the app waters down the Scripture to be "inclusive," it loses both audiences.
3. **Let the product do the work.** The core loop (check-in, verse, interpretation, prayer, tracking) serves any man at any stage. The personalization engine handles the range — not the brand voice.
4. **Imman.faith is the bridge for explorers.** His content brings in men who are faith-curious. The app converts them by being genuinely useful, not by evangelizing.

## 4. Core Tensions We Address

These four tensions are the emotional foundation of ETHOS. Every feature, every verse match, every AI conversation maps back to at least one. Each is framed through the masculine lens of who these men are becoming.

| Tension | The Internal Question | Who Feels This Most | How ETHOS Addresses It |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Leadership vs. Passivity** | "Am I becoming a leader or drifting through life?" | College students without direction, young professionals who feel unprepared, men who know they should step up but do not know how | Daily check-in tags (Courage, Discipline, Laziness) surface the tension. Personalized verse speaks to the gap between who he is and who he is called to be. AI interpretation connects Scripture to specific leadership moments. Growth tracking makes progress visible. |
| **Integrity vs. Compromise** | "Do I have to cut corners to get ahead?" | Young professionals in secular workplaces, students navigating peer pressure, men facing ethical decisions with no one watching | Check-in tags (Pride, Doubt, Fear) match to verses about integrity under pressure. AI conversation provides biblical framework for specific ethical dilemmas. Daily prayer grounds the commitment before the day starts. |
| **Purity vs. Temptation** | "Why can't I stop falling?" | College men fighting porn/lust, men in cycles of sin and shame, anyone who knows what is right but keeps choosing wrong | Check-in tag (Lust) triggers grace-oriented Scripture without condemnation. AI conversation meets him in the shame with truth and a path forward. Growth tracking over weeks shows that the falls are getting less frequent — progress he cannot see day-to-day. |
| **Fatherhood vs. Absence** | "Will I become the man my future family deserves?" | Men in serious relationships, men thinking about marriage and children, men whose own fathers were absent and who are determined to be different | Check-in tags (Discipline, Courage, Anger) connect to the long game of who he is building himself to be. AI interpretation frames daily choices as preparation for future responsibility. The vision of fatherhood becomes a daily motivator, not a distant concept. |

### Interview Questions

**Faith and identity (qualify as primary ICP):**
1. How often do you read Scripture in a typical week? What does that look like?
2. Do you consider yourself someone actively trying to grow in your faith? What does that mean to you day-to-day?
3. Do you have any system or routine for spiritual growth right now? What works? What does not?

**Pain point validation:**
4. When you face a real struggle — temptation, anger, doubt — where do you go for guidance?
5. Do you feel like there is a gap between Sunday and the rest of the week? What does that gap look like?
6. Have you tried faith apps before? What did you like? What made you stop using them?

**Product-market fit signals:**
7. If an app asked you to honestly check in each morning with what you are facing — lust, doubt, anger, whatever — and then gave you one Bible verse matched to that struggle with an AI that helped you understand and apply it, would you use it daily?
8. Would you pay $9.99/month for a tool that kept you accountable to becoming the man God intended you to be? Why or why not?
9. What would make you delete a faith app within the first week?

**Masculine framing validation:**
10. Do existing Christian apps feel like they were made for you? Why or why not?
11. What does it mean to you to "be a man" in the context of your faith?
12. Is there a struggle you deal with that you do not talk about at church or with friends? What keeps you silent?

| Most-used check-in tags | Which struggles are most active? | Track for verse matching prioritization |
| Growth tracking engagement | Are men reviewing their progress? | > 30% weekly check |
| Share rate | Is the product generating organic reach? | > 10% of active users share monthly |

### How to Confirm We Are Reaching the Right Men

**We are reaching the primary ICP if:**
- Interviewees describe ETHOS as "the thing that keeps me accountable" or "the tool that helps me become who God wants me to be"
- They check in honestly — tagging Lust, Anger, Doubt — not just safe tags
- Retention is highest among men who check in 5+ days/week
- They describe the app in masculine, action-oriented terms — not soft, comfort-oriented language
- Paid conversion correlates with daily check-in frequency

**We are missing the mark if:**
- Users describe the app as a "Bible reader" or "devotional app" (we are more than that)
- Check-in tags cluster around safe options (Gratitude, Discipline) and avoid the hard ones (Lust, Anger, Shame)
- The AI conversations are surface-level — no vulnerability, no real struggles
- Paid conversion is below 3%
- Users churn after the novelty wears off (day 14-21 cliff) — means the habit did not form
