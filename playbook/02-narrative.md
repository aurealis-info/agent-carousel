# Narrative Playbook

The strategist picks a narrative pattern, places the climax, and structures pacing so the reader swipes through to the CTA.

## Core narrative patterns

- **3-act** (Setup, Confrontation, Resolution) — slides 1-2 setup, 3-7 escalating problem + friction, 8-10 synthesis + CTA.
- **5-act** — same as 3-act with explicit climax + falling action; optimal for complex or contrarian theses.
- **Ladder (value ladder)** — rigid utility progression; top-level actionable advice → deeper conceptual → pure promotion at base.
- **Reveal** — progressive disclosure; withhold the core mechanism until late; sharp dopamine release on discovery.
- **Principle-stack** — sequential dependency; comprehension of slide N requires slide N-1; demands sequential reading; reinforces creator authority.
- **Story-arc** — personal/third-person narrative escalating through conflict → revelation → transferable lesson + CTA.

## Climax placement

The climax carries the heaviest intellectual payload or paradigm-shifting insight. **Never on the final slide.** Two valid positions:

- **Midpoint** — sunk-cost commitment locks the reader in for the back half.
- **Slide N-1** — peak emotional resonance immediately before the CTA. Most common shape for download-CTA carousels.

## Pacing — fluctuating density

Information density must shift violently slide-to-slide. Habituation produces skimming. Recommended rhythm for an 8-10 slide carousel:

- Slide 1: maximum brevity. Lower the entry barrier.
- Slide 2: context. 1-2 short sentences.
- Slides 3-6: high density. Bullets, short paragraphs, named principles.
- A "quiet reset" slide following dense educational blocks: single sentence + massive white space.
- Slide N-1: extreme brevity for the hero statement.
- Slide N: structured CTA density.

**Hard rule:** never stack 3+ "loud" (high-density) slides consecutively. Reader's optical processing exhausts by slide 4.

## The 5-slide chassis — short download-CTA carousels

For app-download CTAs, a 5-slide chassis often outperforms 8-10 slide arcs on save-rate and completion. The shorter chassis trades narrative depth for completion certainty: every slide does exactly one job, and the swipe-cost from one to the next is low enough that completion stays above ~60%.

| Slide | Job | Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Hook | Stop scroll in <3s; self-select audience | 2-6 words. No app mention. Identity/curiosity gap. |
| 2 — Value 1 | Apply-today usefulness | ~40 words. Second person. No app mention. |
| 3 — Value 2 | Deepen value; aphorism close | Last line should be screenshottable. No app mention. |
| 4 — App tie-in | Bridge: name the in-app surface that does this daily | First app mention. Conversational, not salesy. Names a *specific* surface. |
| 5 — CTA + screenshot | Single ask + visual proof | Repeats surface from slide 4. Screenshot shows that exact feature. App name in headline. Strip secondary asks. |

Use this chassis when:
- The app has well-defined surfaces (Daily X, Streak, Y feed, Z dashboard) that map to topics 1:1
- Save-rate / share-rate is the optimization target (ICP wants reference content)
- Brand has a strict register that long arcs dilute

Don't stretch a chassis topic to 8 slides. If the topic genuinely needs more substance, it's two carousels (one shipped today, one queued).

## CTA-bridge slide architecture

The slide immediately before the CTA bridges from passive education to active transaction. Required elements:

1. **Validate the climax** just delivered.
2. **Name the specific "missing piece"** — the execution barrier the reader cannot solve manually.
3. **Position the app as automated execution** of the theory just taught. Not a sales pitch — a logical necessity.

Structural template: "You now understand [X]. But executing [X] daily requires [friction the app removes]."

## CTA slide architecture

The final slide must aggressively differ from a generic "follow me for tips" ending. Required elements:

- **Hard visual break** — typically inverted color palette from preceding slides (light bg → dark bg, or reverse). Signals end of narrative sequence.
- **One kinetic action** — Download <App Name>. Strip ALL secondary asks (no "save this," "tag a friend," "comment thoughts," "follow for more").
- **App name in headline** — explicitly state the app being promoted.

## Five example narrative arcs

### Arc 1 — PAS Ladder (Budgeting Tool)
1. Hook: "Your spreadsheet is making you poor."
2. Problem: Manual tracking relies on willpower; willpower depletes by 3 PM.
3. Agitate: Each missed entry compounds; by Friday you're guessing.
4. Agitate: Guessing fuels the financial-dread cycle.
5. Solution: Zero-friction automated categorization.
6. Climax: Wealth is built on definitive data, not human discipline.
7. CTA-bridge: You know the math. Remove the manual labor.
8. CTA: Download <App Name>.

### Arc 2 — Reveal (Fitness Tracker)
1. Hook: "The 10k steps rule is a marketing myth."
2. Context: Created in 1965 to sell pedometers. No biological basis.
3. Build: What matters is Zone 2 cardio thresholds.
4. Build: 15 minutes in Zone 2 > 10,000 casual steps.
5. Climax/Reveal: The metric to track is sustained heart rate variance.
6. CTA-bridge: Calculating this manually mid-workout is impossible.
7. CTA: Let <App Name> track Zone 2 variance automatically.

### Arc 3 — BAB (Meditation/Sleep)
1. Hook: "Waking up tired after 8 hours of sleep?"
2. Before: Brain stuck in beta-wave loop all night.
3. Before: REM latency blocked. Resting, not recovering.
4. After: Imagine entering deep delta-wave sleep in under 12 minutes.
5. Bridge: The catalyst is binaural acoustic pacing.
6. Climax: Sound fundamentally controls the nervous system.
7. CTA: Get the acoustic pacing audio library. Download <App Name>.

### Arc 4 — Principle-Stack (Investment Platform)
1. Hook: "Compound interest is only half the equation."
2. Principle 1: Compound interest grows capital linearly over time.
3. Principle 2: Inflation degrades purchasing power simultaneously.
4. Principle 3: Real yield = compound interest minus inflation.
5. Climax: If your yield is under 4%, you're actively losing money.
6. CTA-bridge: You need access to assets that outpace the degradation.
7. CTA: Access high-yield fractional real estate. Download <App Name>.

### Arc 5 — Story (Productivity/Focus)
1. Hook: "How I reclaimed 14 hours a week."
2. Setup: In 2023, screen time was 6 hours daily. Burning out.
3. Conflict: Tried gray-scale. Tried hiding apps. Always relapsed.
4. Turning point: Reliance on motivation was the flaw.
5. Climax: I needed algorithmic friction, not human discipline.
6. CTA-bridge: I built a tool that aggressively blocks dopamine loops.
7. CTA: Install the algorithmic friction protocol. Download <App Name>.
