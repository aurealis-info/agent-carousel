  1. 1. You apologize first.

    Not because you were wrong — because the relationship matters more than the point.

  2. 2. You lead your family in prayer before anyone asks.

    Spiritual leadership costs you the most comfortable version of your morning.

  3. 3. You're harder on yourself than on the people you lead.

    Grace given out freely, discipline kept mostly for yourself. Most men have it backwards.

  4. 4. You tell the truth even when it costs you something.

    A leader who manages impressions instead of naming truth isn't leading — he's performing.

  5. 5. You go toward the cost before anyone's watching.

    Sacrifice in private is the tell. The man who serves when no one knows is the one people follow when it matters.
