Step 1

Fruit, not a muscle

The most common fix for self-control is hardware — stricter rules, better systems, more willpower.

It keeps failing. Not because you're weak. Because you're working the wrong category.

Paul doesn't call self-control a skill in Galatians 5. He calls it fruit — the last one listed in the Spirit's harvest.

Fruit doesn't get manufactured. It grows from a root.

You don't force it. You grow it.
