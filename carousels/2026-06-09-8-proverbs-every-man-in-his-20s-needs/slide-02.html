  1. 1. "A slack hand causes poverty, but the hand of the diligent makes rich." (10:4)

    Not wealthy — useful. The diligent man becomes someone people can count on.

  2. 2. "Without counsel plans fail, but with many advisers they succeed." (15:22)

    The man who only trusts himself will only go as far as he can see.

  3. 3. "The soul of the sluggard craves and gets nothing, while the soul of the diligent is richly supplied." (13:4)

    Craving without moving is just hunger with better vocabulary.

  4. 4. "Pride goes before destruction, a haughty spirit before a fall." (16:18)

    The fall doesn't come when you're humble. It comes right after you stop.
