  1. 5. He starts seeing people differently.

    Prayer has a way of replacing contempt with something closer to compassion. You can't stay indifferent to a person you're bringing before God.

  2. 6. His conscience gets louder.

    The closer a man gets to God, the harder it is to keep ignoring the thing he's been tolerating. That's not guilt. That's growth.

  3. 7. His patience finds a floor.

    Not endless patience — a floor. Something to stand on when everything is moving. He's not calm because things are easy. He's calm because he knows who holds it.

  4. 8. He becomes harder to rattle.

    The man who's given his day to God already has somewhere to put what doesn't go according to plan.
