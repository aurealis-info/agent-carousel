Step 4

The humble man doesn't need the credit.

He can do the work when no one's watching. He can serve without an audience. He can lose the argument and be glad the better idea won.

That's not passivity. That's power — the kind that doesn't need to perform. A man who needs credit for everything is a man who can only do things when someone's watching.

  • The work is real whether or not it's acknowledged.
  • The man who needs applause can be controlled by whoever's clapping.

Walk humbly. It's the hardest thing on that list, and the one that holds the other two up.
