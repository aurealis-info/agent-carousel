  1. 1. He apologizes first, even when it costs him the high ground.

    Leadership isn't about being right. It's about being willing to absorb the cost.

  2. 2. He serves when it doesn't help his reputation.

    The man who only shows up when people are watching isn't a leader. He's an actor.

  3. 3. He says hard things to people's faces, gently.

    Not behind their back, not in a passive text. Face to face, in love, with enough respect to be honest.

  4. 4. He shows up when it costs him something.

    Time, money, comfort, sleep. The leader who goes first into the hard thing is the leader people follow.
