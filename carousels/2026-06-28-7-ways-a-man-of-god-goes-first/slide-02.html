  1. 1. He apologizes first.

    Even when he's only partly wrong. Pride waits for the other person to move. A leader doesn't.

  2. 2. He initiates the hard conversation.

    Most men wait. A godly man texts first, calls first, shows up first — before the tension calcifies.

  3. 3. He serves before he's asked.

    The man who notices what needs doing and does it — without announcement — is already leading.

  4. 4. He takes the cost so others don't have to.

    Christ led by absorbing the consequence. That pattern is still the template.
