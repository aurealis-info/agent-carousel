Thing 2

The bitterness you call discernment

You haven’t forgiven him — your dad, your coach, the man who walked away. You keep the account open and call it wisdom.

Hebrews 12:15 names it plainly: a root of bitterness that springs up and defiles many. It’s not protecting you. It’s choking everything else.

  • Name the root specifically. It has a face and a date attached to it.
  • Forgive him — not because he deserves it, but because you can’t afford the cost of carrying it.

Bitterness looks like strength until you realize it’s a chain.
