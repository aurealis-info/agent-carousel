Thing 5

The old identity

Colossians 3:9–10 is past tense: “You have taken off your old self with its practices.” The new man isn’t a patch on old cloth — it’s a replacement.

Most men are renovating the old self instead of burying it. Same patterns, same private compromises, same who-you-are-when-no-one-is-watching — just with better language for it now.

  • What habit or pattern does the old you still own that the new you hasn’t reclaimed?
  • Name it. Then let the past tense in that verse actually be true of you.

You don’t renovate a dead man. You bury him and become the new one.
