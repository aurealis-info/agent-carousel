Step 3

Small debts compound

Every task you postpone is a small debt against next week's version of you. He's the one who pays it, with interest: less time, more weight, a longer list.

The five-minute task left undone becomes the twenty-minute weight you carry around all week.

  • The man who can't finish small things won't be trusted with big ones.
  • Discipline in the small tasks is what earns the big ones.

Pay it now. It only gets more expensive later.
