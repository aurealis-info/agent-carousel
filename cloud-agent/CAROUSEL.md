# CAROUSEL — Shared Craft

Carousels are the format ETHOS leads with — the post a man saves, returns to, screenshots, and sends to a brother. This is the **shared craft standard**: it applies to *every* carousel, whatever the format. For the structure of a specific format, read its file in `formats/`.

---

## Carousel types

A **format** is the copy structure; a **template** is the look. They're independent — a format can have several templates, and you pick/rotate the template per deck (recorded in `type_pairing_id`), keeping the mix balanced over time.

| Format | Use when | Structure file | Template(s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Teaching** | One idea per slide, explained in depth | `formats/01-teaching.md` | `01-editorial-restrained` · `03-annotated-notebook` |
| **List** | A numbered collection of many sharp items | `formats/02-list.md` | `02-editorial-list` |

**Every format:** no CTA slide — the call-to-action is a separate Figma asset appended after rendering — and **the app is never named in the generated copy** (only the brand chrome — a small wordmark/handle — carries the name).

---

## The mission

**Deliver real value first. Hook through that value — a carousel earns attention by being genuinely useful, never by pitching.**

The quality of the free piece is the only honest argument for the paid one. If the value is real, the viewer ends up asking *"what's this app?"* on his own — you never force it. A carousel that doesn't help a man before it asks anything of him is broken — no matter how clean the design.

The viewer's arc: **Stop** (the hook hits) → **Stay** (the value is real and specific) → **Save** (he wants it later) → **Wonder** (what's the app behind this).

---

## Hook engineering (slide 1 is the whole post)

- **Three-word rule** — the value prop must compress to ~three words. If it can't, the topic's too diffuse.
- **Avoid the command frame** — "Stop X / Do Y" triggers resistance. Mirror the pattern he's already in before you direct. Description over prescription.
- **Specificity beats generality** — the Tuesday at 3 PM beats "struggle." Concrete is what makes him think *"that's me."*
- **Self-selection** — make the *right* man stop hard; let the wrong one keep scrolling. Don't try to stop everyone.
- **Faith signal is mandatory — in the hook line, not the subhead.** Slide 1 must self-select *the man trying to grow in his faith*: a faith-coded word (godly, faith, Scripture, God, Christ, the Word, sin, holy, man of God) **in the headline itself**. A subhead may reinforce it but can never be the only carrier — at a glance the post has to read as Christian men's formation, not generic self-improvement. Without it, the hook pulls a generic masculine audience — the wrong room.

---

## Copy psychology (every slide)

- **Identification before prescription** — describe his reality before naming what to do. He has to feel seen before he'll take direction.
- **Conviction-bait, not rage-bait** — strong takes that name a real tension drive saves and comments. Outrage trades the brand. Same pull, different moral root.
- **Don't crowd** — let the dominant element of each slide breathe. (How many ideas live on a slide is a *format* decision — see the format file.)
- **The aphorism close** — the line that travels is the one a man can send to a brother. Build at least one into every deck.
- **Earned Scripture** — when you use a verse, the lines build up to it. Never pasted as decoration.

---

## Anti-patterns (avoid every time)

- **App named in any slide you generate.** The app belongs to the separate CTA asset, never the copy you write. Breaks the value-first contract; trust collapses.
- **Value that isn't useful** — generic advice ("stay disciplined," "trust God") trains the viewer to skip you.
- **Rage-bait or cheap provocation.** Conviction, not contempt.
- **Command hooks with no setup.** Mirror before you direct.
- **Verses dropped without setup.** Scripture lands when the slide built up to it.
