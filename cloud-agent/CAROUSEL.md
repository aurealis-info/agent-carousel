# CAROUSEL — How to Build One

Carousels are the format ETHOS leads with — the post a man saves, returns to, screenshots, and sends to a brother. This doc is the craft standard. It describes *good*, not a script to fill in.

---

## The mission

**Deliver real value first. Hook through that value. The app reveal must feel earned, never pitched.**

The quality of the free piece is the only honest argument for the paid one. If the value is real, the app reveal becomes a question the viewer is already asking ("what's this app?") instead of an ask you force. A carousel that doesn't help a man before it asks anything of him is broken — no matter how clean the design.

The viewer's arc: **Stop** (the hook hits) → **Stay** (the value is real and specific) → **Save** (he wants it later) → **Wonder** (what's the app behind this).

---

## Structure: hook → steps → CTA (dynamic length)

A carousel is **1 hook slide + N step slides + 1 CTA slide**, where **N is 3–5** depending on the topic. Total slides = N + 2. Pick the count the topic actually needs; don't pad to hit a number, don't cram two ideas to save one.

| Slide | Role | App mention? |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hook** — stop the scroll | No |
| 2 … N+1 | **Steps** — one tip/idea each, delivered in depth | No |
| N+2 | **CTA** — the ask + the bridge to the app | Yes (first and only) |

The arc is the contract; the words inside each role are open.

---

## Slide 1 — the Hook

- **The hook line:** 2–6 words, or a short line plus a one-sentence subhead. A pain point or curiosity gap, **not the topic name**. No product, no promise of help — just the line that makes a man pause.
- **Faith signal is mandatory.** Slide 1 must self-select *the man trying to grow in his faith*. Either bake a faith-coded word into the hook (godly, faith, Scripture, God, Christ, the Word) **or** add a one-line subhead that does it. Without it, the hook pulls a generic masculine audience — the wrong room.
- **The pause.** It must arrest in under 3 seconds: contrast, an unfinished thought, a question he can't answer without scrolling.
- **No app mention.**

---

## Slides 2…N+1 — the Steps (one idea each, in depth)

Each step slide is a single tip/idea, developed enough to be genuinely useful on its own. Anatomy (all parts optional except title + body):

- **Step label** — `STEP 1`, `STEP 2`, … (or `TIP 1` …). Auto-numbered.
- **Title** — short, bold, declarative. The idea in a few words (e.g. "ADDRESS THE GIANT BY NAME").
- **Body** — a few lines of narrative or teaching. Write as an array of short lines, not one wall of text; each line is its own beat.
- **Arrow points** — optional `→` bullets that sharpen the application ("→ Name the thing you've been avoiding.").
- **Closing line** — optional one-line landing that travels (screenshottable).

Rules under the format:
- **Second person.** Talk to *him*.
- **One idea per slide.** He's reading on a phone in seconds — give him one thing to take away.
- **Genuinely useful.** He should be able to apply it before he closes the app.
- **Concrete > abstract — every line.** Real situations beat categories.
- **Earned Scripture.** When you use a verse, the lines build up to it. Never pasted as decoration.
- **No app mention** — he's here for the value; respect the contract.

---

## Slide N+2 — the CTA (the bridge)

The only place the app is named. Quiet, not salesy — the value already did the selling. It has three fields, and **getting their roles right matters** (each renders very differently):

- **`cta_distillation`** — a **2–5 word** punchy line. It renders huge, so it must be short (e.g. "Pick up the stone." / "Become him first."). A long sentence here overflows the slide.
- **`cta_subline`** — one normal line: the ask (e.g. "Download ETHOS — link in bio.").
- **`comment_trigger`** — the comment-to-DM instruction, written in full (e.g. `comment "begin" for the link`). This is the highest-converting CTA on Instagram — a DM with the link removes friction.

The bridge must land on a **specific in-app surface** (see `PRODUCT.md` when available — e.g. Daily Anchor, Saved Verses, Streak, Chat Companion). Generic "check out the app" breaks the conversion. The value you just delivered is *one rep* of what ETHOS does daily — make that promise concrete.

## Hook engineering (slide 1 is the whole post)

- **Three-word rule** — the value prop must compress to ~three words. If it can't, the topic's too diffuse.
- **Avoid the command frame** — "Stop X / Do Y" triggers resistance. Mirror the pattern he's already in before you direct. Description over prescription.
- **Specificity beats generality** — the Tuesday at 3 PM beats "struggle." Concrete is what makes him think *"that's me."*
- **Self-selection** — make the *right* man stop hard; let the wrong one keep scrolling. Don't try to stop everyone.

---

## Copy psychology (every slide)

- **Identification before prescription** — describe his reality before naming what to do. He has to feel seen before he'll take direction.
- **Conviction-bait, not rage-bait** — strong takes that name a real tension drive saves and comments. Outrage trades the brand. Same pull, different moral root.
- **One idea per slide.** Don't crowd.
- **The aphorism close** — the last line of a step should be sendable to a brother. That's the line that travels.

---

## Format archetypes (vary these across posts)

Different ways to fill the steps. Each has a different mechanic:

- **Listicle** — "N traits / verses / disciplines." Anticipation + collectability. Highest save-rate; reads as a reference asset.
- **Mistakes list** — "N things men get wrong about…" Identification + relief. Never sneering — brotherly.
- **Uncomfortable questions** — each step a question he can't answer comfortably. Self-confrontation. Use sparingly.
- **Problem / solution** — early steps show the hard/wrong way; later steps the better path. Contrast.
- **Cultural lens** — frame through a film/book/moment he already inhabits. Borrowed attention; the substance is still the pillar.

---

## Anti-patterns (avoid every time)

- **App named before the CTA.** Breaks the value-first contract; trust collapses.
- **A CTA that lectures or sells** ("here's why ETHOS is great"). The value already sold; keep the bridge quiet.
- **Generic CTA** — "check out the app" is not a CTA. Point to a specific in-app surface.
- **Value that isn't useful** — generic advice ("stay disciplined," "trust God") trains the viewer to skip you.
- **Rage-bait or cheap provocation.** Conviction, not contempt.
- **Command hooks with no setup.** Mirror before you direct.
- **Verses dropped without setup.** Scripture lands when the slide built up to it.
- **Overstuffing.** One idea per step; 3–5 steps. If the topic needs more, it's two carousels.
- **A long `cta_distillation`.** It renders huge — keep it to a few words.
