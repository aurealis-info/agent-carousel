The man you are at 2 AM is the one God's working with.

Not the Sunday version. Not the one your coworkers see. The one alone, phone in hand, when nobody's watching.

That guy is who you actually are. The rest is performance.

And here's what most men miss: the hidden stuff doesn't stay hidden. The private tab shapes how you look at her in the daylight. The thing you feed in the dark feeds on you in the light. Nothing rots in secret without seeping out.

The only thing that breaks the grip is light. Confession to one brother who'll pick up the phone. Not a stage. Not a small group icebreaker. One man, eye to eye, who's allowed to ask the hard question.

"Confess your sins to one another, that you may be healed." — James 5:16.

Quit running three versions of yourself. Gym, church, group chat, browser — same guy. The man at the table is the man on the phone, or you're not really either of them yet.
